Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence that focuses on the interaction between computers and human language. The goal of NLP is for computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a way that is both meaningful and useful. Key tasks in NLP include sentiment analysis, translation, and text summarization.

Modern NLP systems rely heavily on transformer models like BERT and GPT. These models use attention mechanisms to understand the context of words in a sentence, allowing for more accurate language representation than previous statistical methods. Tokenization, stemming, and lemmatization are essential preprocessing steps in the NLP pipeline to clean data before feeding it into models.

Applications of NLP are everywhere, from the chatbots on websites to the voice assistants like Siri and Alexa. Sentiment analysis helps companies understand customer feedback by categorizing reviews as positive, negative, or neutral. Named Entity Recognition (NER) allows for the extraction of specific information such as names, dates, and locations from unstructured text.

As language is inherently complex and ambiguous, NLP continues to face challenges in understanding nuances like sarcasm, cultural references, and dialects. Researchers are working on improving the robustness of NLP models to handle diverse linguistic structures and context-dependent meanings.
